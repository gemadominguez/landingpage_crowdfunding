About us

Why Plant a Tree?

TreeOfLife is a non-profit organization dedicated to combating climate change through tree planting. Founded with the mission of creating a greener and more sustainable future, we focus on restoring terrestrial ecosystems and fighting deforestation. In line with the United Nations' 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, we particularly emphasize SDG 13 (Climate Action) and SDG 15 (Life on Land). Through our initiatives, we aim to make a positive environmental impact and support communities, helping to create a healthier planet for future generations.

Reduction of CO₂

A mature tree can absorb between 10 and 40 kg of CO₂ per year and up to 1 ton of CO₂ throughout its lifetime.

Water Cycle

Trees help retain up to 100,000 liters of water per year, preventing droughts and recharging aquifers

Improved Air Quality

An urban tree can filter up to 20 kg of pollutants annually, including harmful particles and gases like sulfur dioxide.

Natural Protection

Trees reduce flood risks by absorbing and slowing down up to 30% of rainfall, acting as natural sponges.

Mental Health

People living near green areas are 20% less likely to suffer from anxiety or depression

Biodiversity

Forests are home to 80% of terrestrial species, including endangered animals.

Climate Regulation

Tropical forests generate more than 40% of global rainfall, helping stabilize weather patterns.

Local economies

Planting trees promotes sustainability and environmental health, driving long-term economic growth. Our NGO supports these efforts.
